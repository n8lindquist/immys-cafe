## Ingredients

### Blend first
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- 3/4 cup rolled oats, old-fashioned, dry

### Wet
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 120g egg whites
- 1 cup 2% low-fat cottage cheese
- 2 eggs
- 1 tbsp vanilla extract

### Add in
- 1 tsp baking powder
- Pinch of salt
- Cinnamon or pumpkin spice, to taste

## Instructions

1. Blend the chia seeds and dry oats together until you get a rough oat flour. Set aside.
2. In a blender, combine maple syrup, egg whites, cottage cheese, eggs, and vanilla. Blend until smooth. No lumps.
3. Mix the oat-chia flour, baking powder, salt, and spice into the blended wet mixture. A stand mixer is good here.
4. Let the batter rest for at least 30 minutes before cooking. It will be thick. This matters for the texture.
5. Cook in a waffle iron until golden. Makes about 10 waffles, 2 to 3 servings of 4.

## Macros (per serving of 4 waffles)

About 300 calories, about 20g protein.

Want more protein, add more egg whites and cottage cheese. The ratio is forgiving.

## Notes

- Keeps in the fridge up to a week. Toast before eating, don't microwave.
- Freeze them flat and toast directly from frozen. No thawing needed.
- Top with Land O'Lakes spreadable butter and frozen blueberries microwaved for 30 seconds.
- For sweeter, add more maple syrup, or swap in peanut butter, dark chocolate chips, or frozen banana.
- PB Fit powder works if you want peanut butter flavor without much fat.

## Variations

Swap the maple syrup for something else:

- Frozen banana blended into the wet mix
- Dark chocolate chips folded into the batter before cooking
- A tablespoon or two of peanut butter blended into the wet

## How this happened

These are called Immys. Imogen made them.

It started at Trader Joe's. The $4 cottage cheese pancakes looked good because the protein was from a real source, not just powder, and they were pretty good. But $4 for four tiny pancakes once you know what's in them felt like a bad deal.

Spent some time on Reddit finding ratios, tested a few batches, and landed here. Better texture, more fiber from the oats and chia, more complex carbs than the Trader Joe's version. And you get 10 waffles for the cost of ingredients.

Imogen figured out the waffle iron is better than the pan. She was right.
