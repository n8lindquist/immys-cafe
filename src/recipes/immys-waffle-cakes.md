## Ingredients

### Blend First (dry)
- 1 tbsp chia seeds
- ¾ cup rolled oats (old-fashioned, dry)

### Wet
- 1 tbsp maple syrup
- 120g egg whites
- 1 cup 2% low-fat cottage cheese
- 2 eggs
- 1 tbsp vanilla extract

### Add to Combined
- 1 tsp baking powder
- Pinch of salt
- Cinnamon or pumpkin spice, to taste

## Instructions

1. Blend the chia seeds and dry oats together until you have a rough oat flour. Set aside.
2. In a blender, combine maple syrup, egg whites, cottage cheese, eggs, and vanilla. Blend until completely smooth — no cottage cheese lumps.
3. Slowly combine the oat-chia flour, baking powder, salt, and spice into the blended wet mixture. A stand mixer works well here.
4. Let the batter rest for at least 30 minutes before cooking. It will be thick. Don't skip this.
5. Cook in a waffle iron until golden. Makes about 10 waffles — 2 to 3 servings of 4.

## Macros (per serving of 4 waffles)

~300 calories · ~20g protein

Add more egg whites and cottage cheese to increase protein without significantly changing the texture.

## Notes

- **Storage:** Keeps in the fridge up to 1 week. Toast before eating — don't microwave.
- **From frozen:** Freeze flat, toast directly from frozen. No thawing needed.
- **Toppings:** Land O'Lakes spreadable butter + frozen blueberries microwaved 30 seconds.
- **Sweeter:** Increase maple syrup, or add peanut butter, dark chocolate chips, or frozen banana.
- **PB option:** PB Fit powder blends in well if you want peanut butter flavor without the fat.

## Variations

Swap the maple syrup for any of these:

- **Banana** — one serving of frozen banana, blended into the wet mix
- **Chocolate chip** — fold dark chocolate chips into the batter before cooking
- **Peanut butter** — add a tablespoon or two, blended into the wet

## The Story

These started at Trader Joe's. The $4 cottage cheese pancakes had real protein from a whole food source — not powder — and they were pretty good. But $4 for four tiny pancakes felt like a bad deal once you understood what was in them.

Spent some time on Reddit finding ratios, tested a few batches, and landed on this. Better texture, more fiber from the oats and chia, more complex carbs — and you get 10 waffles for the price of ingredients.

Imogen figured out the waffle iron makes them better than the pan. She was right.
